Automation Legend & System Map

Every action in this SOP is tagged to show what's automated, what's manual, and what triggers downstream automations.

How to read this guide: Throughout the SOP, you'll see colored tags on each step. These tell you whether you need to do something, or whether the system handles it automatically.
  • Purple AUTO = the system does this for you — no action needed
  • Orange MANUAL = you need to take this action yourself
  • Green ZUPER SYNC = data flows automatically from Zuper field software to HubSpot
  • Pink CHECKLIST = submit a checklist in Zuper to trigger the next step

Tag Reference

AUTO

Happens automatically via HubSpot workflow. No human action needed.

MANUAL

Requires a team member to take action — update a field, make a call, upload a doc.

TRIGGER

Your manual action kicks off downstream automations. You act, then the system reacts.

ZUPER SYNC

Data synced from Zuper Pro field software. Update in Zuper → auto-syncs to HubSpot.

CHECKLIST

Completed by submitting a Zuper checklist (survey complete, construction complete, inspection failure). Submission syncs to HubSpot and triggers automations.

CALCULATED

Auto-calculated timing metric. Computes when date fields are populated.

NOTIFICATION

Auto-sent customer SMS + Email notification triggered by a status change.

Site Survey

Scheduling, conducting, and documenting site surveys with full automation mapping.

At a glance: The site survey is the first field visit after a deal is sold. Here's what happens:
  • Survey gets scheduled (via Zuper or the scheduler) — customer gets a text and email
  • Crew goes to the site, takes photos and measurements, uploads everything
  • Crew marks the job complete in Zuper — HubSpot automatically advances the deal to Design
  • If there are issues (bad roof, HOA, etc.), flag them before marking complete

Design & Engineering

Full engineering design process from survey handoff through design completion and stage advance to Permitting.

Design Dashboard

At a glance: After the site survey, the design team creates the solar system plan. Here's the flow:
  • Vishtik (design vendor) creates the initial design and uploads it
  • Design Lead reviews → customer approves the design via PandaDoc (the "DA")

  • When stamped plans are uploaded, the deal automatically advances to Permitting

Permitting

Permit application, tracking, and approval process. One half of the RTB dual-gate.

Permitting Dashboard

At a glance: Permitting is one of two gates before construction can start (the other is Interconnection).
  • Normal path: permit goes straight from "Ready for Permitting" → "Submitted to AHJ" (the "Submitted to Customer" and "Customer Signature" steps only trigger when the AHJ requires it — based on a property on the AHJ record associated to the deal)
  • When the permit is issued, someone manually sets the status to "Permit Issued"
  • This is a manual step — the AHJ doesn't update HubSpot directly
  • Once both permit AND interconnection are approved (and DA invoice paid), the deal moves to Ready To Build

Monitor AHJ response. If rejected, the revision path is followed: "Permit Rejected - Needs Revision" → "Design Revision In Progress" → "Revision Ready To Resubmit" → "Resubmitted to AHJ".

RTB Gate (Ready To Build)

The multi-condition gate that controls when a project can move to construction. Permit issued, interconnection approved, AND DA invoice paid must all be satisfied.

BOM Tool

At a glance: A project can't start construction until three things are true:
  • ✅ Permit issued — the local building authority approved the plans
  • ✅ Interconnection approved — the utility approved the grid connection

When all three are met, the deal automatically moves to Ready To Build. If any are missing, it goes to "RTB - Blocked" with the reason tracked.
