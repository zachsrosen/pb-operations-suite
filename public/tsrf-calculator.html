📐 Seasonal TSRF Breakdown
Season Est. TSRF Peak POA Peak DC
How to read this table: All DC output uses your annual TSRF applied uniformly — the orientation (azimuth/pitch) drives seasonal variation through solar geometry, not through TSRF adjustment. The Est. TSRF column shows what fraction of optimal-site radiation this orientation captures each season. Peak DC reflects clear-sky POA × annual TSRF × temperature derating. For precise seasonal TSRF values, export monthly TSRF from OpenSolar directly.
Methodology: Clear-sky GHI → Haurwitz model. DNI/DHI decomposition → Erbs model. Tilted-surface irradiance → isotropic sky transposition. Module temperature → NOCT model (NOCT = 45 °C, STC = 25 °C). Half-hour resolution, 04:00–20:00 solar time. DC(t) = array_kW × (POA(t)/1000) × annualTSRF × tempDerate(t). Annual TSRF is applied uniformly to every hour — the correct treatment of a single composite annual metric that bundles orientation + shading + soiling. Seasonal variation in output is driven entirely by solar geometry (POA calculation), which is inherently azimuth/pitch/latitude-dependent. Adjusting TSRF further by season/orientation would double-count the orientation component and inflate off-axis orientations.
