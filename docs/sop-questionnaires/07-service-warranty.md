# SOP Questionnaire: Service & Warranty Team

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell someone new who's going to handle service calls starting next week.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for?

1.3. How is the service team structured? Who handles intake, triage, dispatch, and resolution?

1.4. Is service a dedicated team, or do install crews also handle service? How is that balanced?

1.5. What's the boundary between service and other teams? (Ops, PM, design, etc.)

1.6. What non-service tasks do you end up handling that aren't officially your job?

---

## Section 2: Service Request Intake

2.1. How do service requests come in? List every channel. (Phone, email, HubSpot ticket, Zuper, website form, customer text, internal referral, monitoring alert, etc.)

2.2. For each channel: who receives the request, and what happens first?

2.3. What information do you need from the customer to understand the issue? What's the minimum to triage?

2.4. How do you log the initial request? What system? What fields/details?

2.5. How do you access the customer's original project history? (What system was installed, when, by whom)

2.6. What questions do you ask the customer during initial intake? Is there a script or checklist?

2.7. How do you handle requests that come in after hours or on weekends?

2.8. What's the volume of service requests? Per day, per week?

2.9. What are the most common reasons customers contact you?

---

## Section 3: Triage & Prioritization

3.1. How do you assess the severity/priority of a service request? What criteria?

3.2. What's your priority scale? (Emergency, urgent, standard, low) Define each level with examples.

3.3. What constitutes an emergency? What response time is expected?

3.4. How do you triage remotely? What diagnostics can you do without going on-site? (Monitoring portals, customer-provided photos, etc.)

3.5. What monitoring systems do you have access to? (Enphase Enlighten, SolarEdge, Tesla, etc.)

3.6. How do you determine if the issue is PB's responsibility vs the customer's vs the equipment manufacturer's?

3.7. How do you handle requests that are clearly not covered under warranty? What do you tell the customer?

3.8. How do you handle requests where the original install was done by a different company?

3.9. What percentage of service requests can be resolved remotely without a truck roll?

3.10. What are the most common misdiagnoses? Where do things get triaged wrong?

---

## Section 4: Warranty & Coverage Determination

4.1. What warranty coverage does PB provide? What's the standard workmanship warranty?

4.2. How do you look up warranty status for a specific project? What system?

4.3. What manufacturer warranties apply to different components? (Panels, inverters, batteries, racking, etc.)

4.4. How do you file a manufacturer warranty claim? Walk us through the process for the top 2–3 manufacturers.

4.5. What documentation is required for a warranty claim? (Photos, serial numbers, error codes, etc.)

4.6. What's the typical turnaround on manufacturer warranty replacements?

4.7. How do you handle a warranty claim that gets denied by the manufacturer?

4.8. What's the process for determining if something is warranty vs billable? Who makes the final call?

4.9. How do you communicate warranty status and expectations to the customer?

4.10. What warranty tracking exists? (Spreadsheet, database, system?)

---

## Section 5: Scheduling & Dispatch

5.1. How do you schedule a service call? What system? What's the process?

5.2. How do you decide who handles the service call? (Dedicated service crew, nearest crew, original installer, etc.)

5.3. How do you communicate the service appointment to the customer? When? Through what channel?

5.4. What information does the service tech need before going on-site?

5.5. What parts or equipment need to be pre-staged for the service call?

5.6. How do you handle same-day or emergency dispatch?

5.7. What's the target timeline from request to resolution for each priority level?

5.8. How do you balance service calls against the install schedule?

5.9. What's the rescheduling process if a service call needs to be moved?

---

## Section 6: On-Site Service & Diagnostics

6.1. What does the service tech do first when arriving on-site? Walk us through the process.

6.2. What diagnostic tools and equipment does the service tech bring?

6.3. What's the diagnostic process for the most common issues? Walk us through 3–5 typical scenarios:
   - System not producing / underperforming
   - Inverter fault or offline
   - Panel-level issue (critter damage, physical damage, etc.)
   - Monitoring not communicating
   - Battery system issue
   - Roof leak at penetration point

6.4. How does the tech determine root cause vs symptom?

6.5. What decisions can the tech make on-site vs what requires calling the office?

6.6. How does the tech communicate findings and status back to the office during the visit?

6.7. What happens when the tech can't resolve the issue in one visit?

6.8. What documentation does the tech produce on-site? (Photos, notes, measurements, etc.)

6.9. How does the tech close out the visit with the homeowner?

---

## Section 7: Resolution & Follow-Up

7.1. What does "resolved" mean? How do you verify the issue is actually fixed?

7.2. How do you update the service ticket/record when work is complete?

7.3. What follow-up communication does the customer receive after resolution?

7.4. How do you track that the fix is holding? (Follow-up monitoring, customer callback, etc.)

7.5. What's the process for recurring issues at the same site?

7.6. How do you close out a service ticket in HubSpot and/or Zuper?

7.7. What's the process when a service call reveals that a larger repair or system modification is needed?

---

## Section 8: Detach & Reset (D&R)

8.1. How do D&R requests come in? What triggers them?

8.2. What's the full D&R process from request to system re-energized?

8.3. How do you coordinate with the roofing contractor? What information do they need?

8.4. How do you handle equipment storage between detach and reset?

8.5. How do you inspect panels and equipment before reinstall? What could disqualify a panel from going back up?

8.6. What documentation do you produce for D&R? (Pre-detach photos, equipment inventory, post-reset commissioning)

8.7. What's the typical timeline for a D&R project?

8.8. What are the biggest risks or common problems with D&R?

---

## Section 9: Customer Communication

9.1. How do you communicate with customers throughout the service process? Map every touchpoint.

9.2. What scripts or templates do you use? Where are they stored?

9.3. How do you handle an angry or frustrated customer? What's the de-escalation approach?

9.4. How do you set timeline expectations? What do you say when you don't know how long something will take?

9.5. How do you explain technical issues to non-technical customers?

9.6. When a customer issue is outside PB's scope (tree trimming, utility issue, etc.), how do you redirect them?

9.7. How do you handle customers who call repeatedly about the same issue?

---

## Section 10: Monitoring & Proactive Service

10.1. Do you proactively monitor customer systems for issues? How?

10.2. What monitoring platforms do you use? (Enphase, SolarEdge, Tesla, Generac, etc.)

10.3. What alerts trigger a proactive service response vs something you just note?

10.4. How often do you review monitoring for existing customers?

10.5. Is there a process for reaching out to customers before they contact you?

10.6. What patterns in monitoring data indicate impending problems?

---

## Section 11: Inventory & Parts for Service

11.1. What spare parts and replacement equipment do you keep in stock for service?

11.2. Where are service parts stored? How do you track inventory?

11.3. How do you handle a service call when you don't have the needed part?

11.4. What's the process for ordering replacement parts? Who approves? How long does it take?

11.5. How do you handle discontinued products that need replacement?

11.6. What does the return/RMA process look like for defective equipment?

---

## Section 12: Tools & Systems

12.1. List every tool/system you use. For each: purpose, criticality (1–5), how well it works (1–5).

12.2. How do you track service requests end-to-end? What's the system of record?

12.3. What reporting do you produce? For whom? How often?

12.4. What data is missing that would help you do your job better?

12.5. What manual processes could be automated?

---

## Section 13: Exceptions & Edge Cases

13.1. What are the most unusual service situations you've dealt with?

13.2. How do you handle service for a system that PB didn't originally install?

13.3. How do you handle critter damage (squirrels, birds nesting under panels)?

13.4. What do you do when storm damage has affected multiple customers at once?

13.5. How do you handle a customer who wants to expand or modify their existing system?

13.6. What happens when you discover code violations on the original install during a service call?

14.1. What KPIs or metrics are you measured on? (Response time, resolution rate, repeat call rate, etc.)

14.2. What does a "good week" look like?

14.3. What metrics would help you improve service quality?

14.4. How do you track customer satisfaction with service?

15.1. What would a new service team member need to learn first?

15.4. What tribal knowledge isn't documented?

16.1. What's the single biggest pain point in service right now?

16.2. What process exists that nobody follows?

16.3. What works well in the current service workflow?
