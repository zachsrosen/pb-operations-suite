# SOP Questionnaire: Sales Team

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a new hire on their first day. That's the level of detail we're after.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. In your own words, what are the top 3–5 things you are personally responsible for every day?

1.3. What parts of the project lifecycle do you touch? Where does your involvement start, and where does it end?

1.4. Who do you report to, and who (if anyone) reports to you?

1.5. Are there responsibilities that "officially" belong to someone else but you end up handling regularly? What are they, and why does that happen?

1.6. Are there things you're supposed to do that you rarely have time for? What gets deprioritized and why?

---

## Section 2: Lead Intake & Qualification

2.1. How do new leads enter your pipeline? List every source (website form, phone call, referral, canvasser, partner, etc.).

2.2. For each source, what information typically comes with the lead? What's usually missing that you have to chase down?

2.3. What makes a lead "qualified" in your mind? What specific criteria do you check before investing time?

2.4. What are your automatic disqualifiers — things that make you immediately pass or reject a lead?

2.5. Walk us through exactly what you do when a new lead hits your queue. Step by step, from notification to first action.

2.6. How do you prioritize when you have multiple new leads at once? What factors matter most?

2.7. What's the typical response time from lead received to first contact? What's the target? What actually happens?

2.8. How do you log your initial contact in HubSpot? What fields do you fill in, what notes do you write?

2.9. What happens to leads that aren't ready now but might be later? How do you track and nurture them?

2.10. What's the most common reason a lead falls through the cracks? What would prevent that?

---

## Section 3: Customer Communication & Relationship

3.1. What's your first communication with a new customer? Phone? Email? Text? Walk us through that first touchpoint.

3.2. What information do you need to gather from the customer during your initial conversations? Is there a checklist?

3.3. How do you set customer expectations about the solar process timeline, cost, and what will happen at each stage?

3.4. What questions do customers ask most frequently? How do you answer them?

3.5. What objections or concerns come up most often? What are your go-to responses?

3.6. How often do you check in with customers during the process? Is there a set cadence, or is it ad hoc?

3.7. How do you handle a customer who goes silent or becomes unresponsive?

3.8. What's the escalation path when a customer is upset or threatening to cancel? Who do you involve, and when?

3.9. Do you use any templates (email, text, etc.) for customer communication? Which ones? Where are they stored?

3.10. What customer communication do you wish was automated that currently isn't?

---

## Section 4: Site Survey Scheduling

4.1. When in the sales process do you schedule the site survey? What needs to be true before you schedule it?

4.2. Walk us through the exact steps to schedule a site survey — what system do you use, what do you click, what info do you enter?

4.3. How do you coordinate the survey time with the customer? What tools do you use (phone, email, calendar link)?

4.4. What information does the survey crew need from you before they go out? How do you communicate it?

4.5. What's the ideal turnaround from "customer says yes" to "survey completed"? What actually happens?

4.6. What causes survey reschedules? How often does that happen? How do you handle it with the customer?

4.7. Are there situations where you skip a site survey or do a virtual/remote one? When and why?

4.8. What do you do while the survey is happening? Do you attend? Monitor remotely?

4.9. How do you know when the survey is complete? Who notifies you and how?

4.10. What commonly goes wrong with survey scheduling, and what would fix it?

---

## Section 5: Proposal & Design Approval (DA)

5.1. After the survey is done, what happens next on your end? What data do you review from the survey results?

5.2. How is the system design / proposal created? What's your involvement in that process?

5.3. How do you present the proposal to the customer? In person, video call, email, PandaDoc?

5.4. What does a successful proposal presentation look like? Walk us through the conversation.

5.5. What are the most common reasons a customer doesn't approve the design on first presentation?

5.6. When a customer wants changes to the design (more panels, different placement, battery added, etc.), what's the process? Who do you talk to? How long does it take?

5.7. How do you track whether a DA has been sent, viewed, and signed? What tool/system?

5.8. What's the typical time from "DA sent" to "DA signed"? What's the longest it's taken? What causes delays?

5.9. What do you do when a customer is stalling on signing? What's the follow-up cadence?

5.10. Once DA is signed, what do you do next? What systems do you update? Who do you notify?

---

## Section 6: Sales → Operations Handoff

6.1. What is the formal moment that a deal transitions from "your project" to "ops' project"? What triggers it?

6.2. What specific information must be in the deal record before you hand it off? Is there a checklist?

6.3. What fields in HubSpot do you own and are responsible for completing before handoff?

6.4. What's the most common piece of information that's missing or wrong at handoff? What are the consequences?

6.5. Do you have a conversation (verbal or written) with the PM or ops team during handoff, or is it purely system-based?

6.6. What does a "perfect" handoff look like? Describe one that went smoothly.

6.7. What does a messy handoff look like? What went wrong and what was the impact?

6.8. After handoff, what's your ongoing involvement? Do customers still call you? Do you stay in the loop?

6.9. How are you notified when key milestones happen on your deals post-handoff (permit approved, install scheduled, PTO granted)?

6.10. What would you change about the handoff process if you could change one thing?

---

## Section 7: HubSpot & Deal Record Management

7.1. Walk us through how you use HubSpot day-to-day. What's your typical workflow when you log in?

7.2. What views, filters, or dashboards do you use most? Do you have custom views set up?

7.3. What deal properties do you interact with most frequently? Which ones are confusing or redundant?

7.4. How do you log activities (calls, emails, meetings, notes)? How consistently?

7.5. What tasks do you create and for whom? How do you use the HubSpot task system?

7.6. What reporting do you pull from HubSpot? Weekly, monthly, ad hoc?

7.7. What's the most frustrating thing about HubSpot from your perspective?

7.8. Are there workarounds you use because the system doesn't do what you need? Describe them.

7.9. What data do you wish you had access to that you currently don't?

7.10. If you could automate one part of your HubSpot workflow, what would it be?

---

## Section 8: Tools & Systems

8.1. List every tool/system you use in a typical week (HubSpot, Google Workspace, Zuper, phone system, etc.).

8.2. For each tool, rate how critical it is (1=nice to have, 5=can't do my job without it) and how well it works (1=broken, 5=great).

8.3. What information do you have to enter in multiple places? Where does duplication happen?

8.4. Are there tools other teams use that you wish you had access to or visibility into?

8.5. How do you access customer documents (contracts, proposals, utility bills, etc.)? Where are they stored?

---

## Section 9: Exceptions, Escalations & Edge Cases

9.1. What are the most common exceptions or "weird situations" that don't fit the normal process? How do you handle each one?

9.2. New construction projects — how does the process differ from a standard residential retrofit?

9.3. Commercial projects — anything different in your sales process for commercial vs residential?

9.4. When a customer cancels, what's the process? What systems do you update? Who do you notify?

9.5. When you need help or don't know the answer, who do you go to? What's the escalation chain?

9.6. Describe a scenario that caused a lot of pain in the last 6 months. What happened, and what would have prevented it?

---

## Section 10: Metrics & Goals

10.1. What KPIs or metrics are you personally measured on?

10.2. What numbers do you track yourself, even if nobody asks for them?

10.3. How do you define a "good week" vs a "bad week"?

10.4. What data would help you sell more effectively that you don't currently have?

---

## Section 11: Training & Onboarding

11.1. If a new salesperson started tomorrow, what would they need to know on day one?

11.2. What took you the longest to learn? What do you wish someone had told you earlier?

11.3. Is there any existing training documentation? Where is it, and how accurate is it?

11.4. What parts of the job are the hardest to explain to someone new?

---

## Section 12: Open Floor

12.1. What's the single biggest inefficiency in your current process?

12.2. What process or rule exists that nobody follows? Why?

12.3. What works really well that we should make sure we don't lose or change?

12.4. If you could change one thing about how PB runs the sales process, what would it be?

12.5. Is there anything we didn't ask about that you think is critical to document?

---

*Thank you for taking the time. Your answers directly shape the SOPs that everyone will rely on — the more detail you provide, the more useful those documents will be.*
