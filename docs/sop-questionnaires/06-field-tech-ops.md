# SOP Questionnaire: Field / Tech Ops (Installation Crews)

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a brand new crew member or apprentice on their first day in the field.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Team Structure

1.1. What is your job title / role, and how long have you been at PB?

1.2. What's the crew structure? How many people per crew? What are the roles (lead, electrician, laborer, etc.)?

1.3. What are you personally responsible for on a job site?

1.4. If you're a crew lead: what decisions can you make on-site without calling the office? What requires a call?

1.5. How are crews formed? Is it the same team every day or does it rotate?

1.6. What skills or certifications does each crew member need?

1.7. What's the experience range on your crew? How do you handle training newer members on-site?

---

## Section 2: Pre-Job Preparation

2.1. How and when do you find out about tomorrow's job(s)? What system? What notification?

2.2. What information do you receive about the job before you go out? (Address, plan set, BOM, customer notes, special instructions)

2.3. Where do you access that information? (Zuper, printed packet, text from ops, etc.)

2.4. What do you review the night before or morning of? What are you looking for?

2.5. How do you verify you have the right materials loaded for the job? Who's responsible for loading?

2.6. When do you pick up materials? Where? How early?

2.7. What does your typical morning look like before leaving for the job site? (Meeting time, loading, travel)

2.8. What information do you wish you had before arriving at a job site that you currently don't get?

2.9. How do you handle it when the plan set or BOM seems wrong or incomplete before you leave?

---

## Section 3: Arrival & Site Setup

3.1. What do you do first when you arrive at a job site? Walk us through the first 30 minutes.

3.2. How do you introduce yourself to the homeowner? What do you tell them about the day?

3.3. What site assessment do you do before starting work? (Roof condition check, access verification, safety check)

3.4. What safety setup do you perform? (Cones, ladder placement, harness points, etc.)

3.5. How do you handle a site that looks different from what the plan set shows?

3.6. What makes you stop and call the office before starting? What's a show-stopper?

3.7. How do you handle a homeowner who has concerns or asks questions you can't answer?

3.8. Where do you set up your work area? How do you protect the customer's property?

---

## Section 4: Roof Work & Racking Installation

4.1. Walk us through the roof mounting process step by step. From laying out mounts to rails being ready for panels.

4.2. How do you determine attachment point locations? Do you follow the plan set exactly, or do you adapt on-site?

4.3. What roof types do you work with? (Comp shingle, tile, metal, flat/TPO, etc.) How does the process differ for each?

4.4. How do you find rafters? What method/tools?

4.5. What do you do when you can't find a rafter where the plan says one should be?

4.6. How do you handle roof penetrations and waterproofing? What products do you use?

4.7. What are the most common problems you encounter during racking installation?

4.8. How do you verify that the racking is level and properly aligned?

4.9. What quality checks do you do on the racking before moving to panel installation?

---

## Section 5: Panel Installation

5.1. Walk us through panel installation step by step. From first panel to last panel clipped in.

5.2. How do you get panels up to the roof? What methods for different roof heights?

5.3. How do you handle panel orientation (portrait vs landscape)?

5.4. How do you manage microinverter or optimizer installation alongside panels?

5.5. What's the process for wiring strings? How do you manage cable routing?

5.6. How do you handle panel grounding?

5.7. What quality checks do you do during panel installation?

5.8. What's the most common mistake made during panel installation? How do you prevent it?

---

## Section 6: Electrical Work

6.1. Walk us through the electrical side of the install — from DC wiring on the roof to the final AC connection.

6.2. How do you run conduit from the roof to the inverter/disconnect location?

6.3. How do you install the inverter? What varies by inverter type (string vs micro)?

6.4. How do you install the rapid shutdown system?

6.5. How do you handle the main panel work? (Breaker installation, bus bar connections, etc.)

6.6. What do you do when the main panel is full or doesn't have capacity? (Sub-panel, load center upgrade, etc.)

6.7. How do you install the production meter / monitoring system?

6.8. How do you handle battery installation when it's part of the project?

6.9. What testing do you do before energizing the system?

6.10. How do you handle the utility disconnect and AC disconnect installation?

6.11. What's the process for system commissioning? Who does it?

---

## Section 7: Documentation & Photos

7.1. What photos do you take during installation? List every required photo.

7.2. At what stages do you take photos? (Before, during, after — be specific)

7.3. What system do you upload photos to? How? From what device?

7.4. What other documentation do you produce on-site? (Inspection cards, checklists, labels, etc.)

7.5. What labels are you required to place? Where? (NEC labels, rapid shutdown, disconnect labeling)

7.6. How do you fill out the completion report or job closeout paperwork?

7.7. What information does the office need from you at end of day?

---

## Section 8: End of Day & Job Completion

8.1. What does the end-of-day process look like? Walk us through the last hour on a job site.

8.2. How do you clean up the work area? What's the standard?

8.3. How do you secure the system before leaving? (Do you leave it energized or off?)

8.4. What final walkthrough do you do with the homeowner?

8.5. What do you say to the homeowner when you leave? Do you set expectations for next steps?

8.6. How do you report job completion to the office? What system? What details?

8.7. What do you do with leftover materials? Where do they go?

8.8. How do you handle a job that can't be finished in one day? What gets communicated, to whom?

---

## Section 9: Problem Situations

9.1. What do you do when you discover roof damage (soft spots, rot, leaks) during installation?

9.2. What do you do when equipment arrives damaged or is the wrong product?

9.3. How do you handle it when the plan set doesn't match the site reality?

9.4. What happens when a customer is upset, confrontational, or asks you to stop work?

9.5. How do you handle a situation where you realize the design won't work as planned?

9.6. What do you do when the weather changes mid-job? What's the cutoff for wind, rain, heat?

9.7. How do you handle a safety incident or injury on-site?

9.8. What's the process when you accidentally cause property damage?

9.9. How do you handle when you're missing a part or tool that you need?

9.10. What's the most common unexpected problem you encounter? How often?

---

## Section 10: Safety

10.1. What safety briefing or check happens before each job? Walk us through it.

10.2. What PPE is required? Who provides it? How is compliance enforced?

10.3. What fall protection systems do you use? What are the rules?

10.4. What electrical safety procedures do you follow?

10.5. How do you handle extreme heat/cold conditions? What are the limits?

10.6. What's the process for a safety stop? Who can call it? What happens?

10.7. How are safety incidents reported? What system?

10.8. What safety training have you received? What do you wish you had more of?

10.9. What's the most dangerous part of the job? What controls are in place?

10.10. Are there any safety shortcuts that people take that shouldn't be taken?

---

## Section 11: Inspections (From Your Perspective)

11.1. How do you prepare a site for inspection? What do you check/verify?

11.2. Who attends the inspection? Is it always someone from the crew?

11.3. What does the inspector typically look at? What are they checking?

11.4. What are the most common things inspectors flag? Which are preventable?

11.5. When an inspection fails, how do you handle the corrections?

11.6. What jurisdictions have the pickiest inspectors? What do they care about?

---

## Section 12: Tools & Equipment

12.1. What tools does each crew member carry personally?

12.2. What tools/equipment does the crew share? Where are they stored?

12.3. What specialized equipment do you use? (Impact drivers, conduit benders, wire strippers, multimeters, etc.)

12.4. How are tools maintained? Who's responsible for replacement?

12.5. What vehicles does the crew use? How are they equipped?

12.6. What technology do you use in the field? (Phone, tablet, Zuper app, etc.)

12.7. What tools or equipment do you wish you had?

---

## Section 13: Communication

13.1. How do you communicate with the office during the day? What tool? How often?

13.2. Who do you call when you have a question about the plan set or design?

13.3. Who do you call when you need a decision made about something on-site?

13.4. How do crew members communicate with each other on-site?

13.5. How are schedule changes communicated to you? How much notice do you get?

13.6. What information do you wish the office communicated better?

---

## Section 14: Service Calls (If Applicable)

14.1. Do you handle service/warranty calls in addition to installs? How does that work?

14.2. How do service calls differ from installs in terms of preparation?

14.3. What are the most common service issues you're sent out for?

14.4. What diagnostic process do you follow for a service call?

14.5. What parts/equipment do you bring for service calls?

---

## Section 15: Metrics & Performance

15.1. How is crew performance measured? What metrics matter?

15.2. How many installs per week is the target? What actually happens?

15.3. What slows you down the most?

15.4. What would help you complete jobs faster without cutting corners?

---

## Section 16: Training & Skill Development

16.1. What training did you receive when you started at PB?

16.2. What skills took the longest to develop?

16.3. What training would you want for new crew members that doesn't exist?

16.4. How do you keep up with new products or installation methods?

16.5. What certifications are relevant to your work? (NABCEP, OSHA, electrical license, etc.)

---

## Section 17: Open Floor

17.1. What's the single biggest thing that makes your job harder than it needs to be?

17.2. What's one thing the office could do differently that would make your field work smoother?

17.3. What works really well in the current process?

17.4. If you could change one thing, what would it be?

17.5. What didn't we ask that you think is critical for someone new to know?

---

*Thank you for your time. The field crew's perspective is critical — you're the ones who actually build these systems, and your SOPs need to reflect the reality of what happens on the roof, not just the office's version of it.*
