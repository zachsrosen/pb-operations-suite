# SOP Questionnaire: Admin / Management

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. This questionnaire is for people in leadership, management, and administrative roles who oversee cross-team operations. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what a new manager or admin hire would need to understand about how PB operates at the organizational level.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. What teams or functions do you oversee directly?

1.3. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for?

1.4. What decisions can only you make? What's escalated to you that could theoretically be handled by someone else?

1.5. How do you split your time between strategic work and day-to-day firefighting? What's the ideal vs actual ratio?

1.6. What administrative/operational work consumes your time that you wish could be delegated or automated?

---

## Section 2: Organizational Structure & Accountability

2.1. Draw the org chart from your perspective. Who reports to whom? Where are the dotted lines?

2.2. For each team, who is the single point of accountability? (The person who owns outcomes for that function)

2.3. Where are there gaps in accountability — areas where it's unclear who owns what?

2.4. How do teams communicate with each other? What are the formal channels? What are the informal ones?

2.5. What cross-team handoffs break down most often? Why?

2.6. How are disagreements between teams resolved? Is there a formal escalation path?

2.7. What decisions require your involvement that shouldn't? What should be pushed down?

---

## Section 3: Strategic Planning & Goal Setting

3.1. What are PB's current strategic priorities for this quarter? This year?

3.2. How are company goals communicated to teams? How often? In what format?

3.3. How do team goals roll up to company goals? Is there a formal OKR or KPI structure?

3.4. How often are goals reviewed and adjusted? Who's in the room for those reviews?

3.5. What metrics do you personally watch to gauge company health? Where do you see them?

3.6. What leading indicators tell you when something is going wrong before it becomes a crisis?

---

## Section 4: Reporting & Dashboards

4.1. What reports do you review regularly? (Daily, weekly, monthly) For each: what it shows, who produces it, where it lives.

4.2. What dashboards do you use? Which are most valuable?

4.3. What data do you wish you had that you currently don't?

4.4. What reports exist that nobody looks at? Should they be killed or fixed?

4.5. How do you communicate company performance to the team? What format, what cadence?

4.6. What reporting takes too long to produce? What should be automated?

4.7. When you need an ad-hoc analysis or answer, who do you go to? How long does it take?

---

## Section 5: Meetings & Communication Cadence

5.1. List every recurring meeting you attend or run. For each: who's there, frequency, purpose, duration, format (in-person, video, etc.).

5.2. Which meetings are essential and productive?

5.3. Which meetings should be eliminated, combined, or reformatted?

5.4. What meetings are missing? What coordination doesn't happen that should?

5.5. How do you communicate urgent information to the whole company? To a specific team?

5.6. What's your policy or preference for email vs Slack vs text vs call? When do you use each?

5.7. How do company-wide announcements happen?

---

## Section 6: Hiring, Onboarding & People Management

6.1. What's the hiring process at PB? Walk us through it from "we need someone" to "they start."

6.2. What's the onboarding process for a new hire? Is it documented? Who owns it?

6.3. How long does it take a new hire to be productive? What could shorten that?

6.4. How do you evaluate team member performance? Is there a formal review process?

6.5. What training programs exist? What training should exist but doesn't?

6.6. How do you handle underperformance? What's the process?

6.7. What's the process for role changes, promotions, or lateral moves?

6.8. What's the biggest people/HR challenge you're dealing with right now?

---

## Section 7: Financial Oversight

7.1. What financial metrics do you track? (Revenue, margin, cost per watt, cost per project, etc.)

7.2. How do you track project profitability? At what level of granularity?

7.3. What's the approval process for large purchases or expenditures?

7.4. How do you manage cash flow and payment timing?

7.5. What financial reporting do you review? From whom? How often?

7.6. How do you handle change orders that affect project profitability?

7.7. What cost centers or categories do you wish you had better visibility into?

---

## Section 8: Vendor & Partner Management

8.1. Who are PB's key vendors and partners? (Equipment distributors, subcontractors, design partners, etc.)

8.2. For each key vendor: who manages the relationship, how does ordering work, what are the terms?

8.3. How do you evaluate vendor performance? What triggers switching vendors?

8.4. What vendor issues have caused project delays in the last 6 months?

8.5. How do you manage subcontractor relationships? (Electricians, roofers, etc.)

8.6. What contracts or agreements exist? Where are they stored? Who reviews them?

---

## Section 9: Quality Control & Standards

9.1. What quality standards exist for PB's work? Are they documented?

9.2. How is quality enforced in the field? Who checks?

9.3. How do you handle quality complaints from customers?

9.4. What's the process when a systematic quality issue is identified?

9.5. How do you track inspection pass/fail rates? What's the target?

9.6. What quality-related metrics do you watch?

---

## Section 10: Risk Management & Compliance

10.1. What are the biggest operational risks PB faces? How are they managed?

10.2. What insurance policies does PB carry? Who manages them?

10.3. What licensing and certification requirements must PB maintain? Who tracks compliance?

10.4. How do you handle safety incidents? What's the reporting and investigation process?

10.5. What legal or regulatory risks do you manage?

10.6. How do you handle customer disputes or complaints that escalate to a legal level?

---

## Section 11: Technology & Systems Oversight

11.1. What systems and tools does PB use across the organization? Who's responsible for each?

11.2. What system integrations exist? (HubSpot ↔ Zuper, etc.) Who maintains them?

11.3. What technology investments are planned or needed?

11.4. How do you handle system outages or tool failures?

11.5. What's the process for evaluating and adopting new tools?

11.6. What technology pain points do you hear most from the teams?

11.7. How do you manage user access, permissions, and security across systems?

---

## Section 12: Customer Experience (Macro View)

12.1. How do you define a great customer experience at PB? What does it look like end-to-end?

12.2. Where in the process do customers have the best experience? The worst?

12.3. How do you collect and act on customer feedback?

12.4. What are the most common customer complaints? What patterns do you see?

12.5. How do you measure customer satisfaction? (NPS, reviews, referral rate, etc.)

12.6. What's the process for handling a customer escalation that reaches leadership?

---

## Section 13: Growth & Capacity Planning

13.1. How do you forecast workload and capacity? What tools or methods?

13.2. How do you decide when to hire additional staff for a team?

13.3. How do you manage seasonal fluctuations in demand?

13.4. What bottlenecks constrain PB's ability to grow?

13.5. What geographic expansion or service line changes are being considered?

13.6. How do you evaluate new market opportunities or service offerings?

---

## Section 14: Process Improvement

14.1. How do process changes happen at PB? Who proposes them? Who approves? How are they rolled out?

14.2. What's the biggest process improvement you've made in the last year? What impact did it have?

14.3. What process improvement have you been wanting to make but haven't gotten to?

14.4. How do you gather input from the team about what's working and what isn't?

14.5. How do you ensure process changes actually stick after rollout?

14.6. What processes have been attempted and abandoned? Why didn't they work?

---

## Section 15: Crisis & Escalation Management

15.1. What's the escalation chain for different types of problems? (Customer issue, safety incident, system failure, financial issue, PR problem)

15.2. How do you handle a day where multiple critical issues hit simultaneously?

15.3. What constitutes a "crisis" at PB? Give examples.

15.4. What would you want to know immediately vs what can wait until morning?

15.5. How do you communicate during a crisis to the team? To customers?

---

## Section 16: Tools & Systems (Your Personal Use)

16.1. What tools do you personally use daily? For what?

16.2. What reports, dashboards, or views do you check first thing?

16.3. What information is hardest for you to find when you need it?

16.4. What would your ideal "management dashboard" show you?

---

## Section 17: Training & Knowledge Transfer

17.1. If you were out for a month, who would cover what? Is that documented?

17.2. What knowledge is in your head that isn't written down anywhere?

17.3. What documentation does PB have that's out of date?

17.4. What documentation is missing entirely?

---

## Section 18: Open Floor

18.1. What's the single biggest operational challenge PB faces right now?

18.2. What process or system exists that nobody actually uses as designed?

18.3. What works really well that we should protect and build on?

18.4. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?

18.5. What didn't we ask about that you think is critical to document in our SOPs?

18.6. What would make the SOPs actually useful to the team vs just another document nobody reads?

---

*Thank you for your time. Your cross-team perspective is essential for building SOPs that don't just describe individual roles but capture how PB operates as a whole.*
