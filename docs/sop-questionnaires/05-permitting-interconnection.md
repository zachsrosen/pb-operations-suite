# SOP Questionnaire: Permitting & Interconnection Team

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a new permitting coordinator on their first day.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for?

1.3. How is the permitting/IC team structured? Who handles what? Are there specializations by jurisdiction, region, or type?

1.4. Where does your ownership start and end? What comes to you and from whom? What leaves you and to whom?

1.5. What's the boundary between your work and the design team's work? Between you and the PM?

1.6. What tasks fall on you that shouldn't? What gets pushed to you that belongs elsewhere?

---

## Section 2: Daily Workflow

2.1. Walk us through your typical morning. What do you check first? What systems do you open?

2.2. How do you track and prioritize your active permits and IC applications? What view/dashboard?

2.3. How many active applications are you typically managing at once?

2.4. How do you batch your work? (All submissions in the morning, follow-ups in the afternoon, etc.)

2.5. What's the biggest time sink in your day that you wish you could eliminate?

---

## Section 3: Permit Application — Preparation

3.1. What triggers you to start the permit application process for a project? What notification or status change?

3.2. What documents and information do you need before you can submit a permit? List everything.

3.3. Where do you get each of those items? What system? From whom?

3.4. What's commonly missing or incomplete when you receive a project for permitting? How do you handle it?

3.5. How do you determine the specific requirements for a given AHJ? Where is that knowledge stored?

3.6. Do different jurisdictions require different document sets? Give examples of the biggest variations.

3.7. What application forms do you fill out? Are they digital or paper? How do you access them?

3.8. What fees are involved? How are they paid? Who authorizes payment?

3.9. How do you prepare the plan set for permit submission? (Any modifications, cover sheets, stamps needed?)

3.10. How long does permit preparation typically take per project?

---

## Section 4: Permit Application — Submission

4.1. How do you submit permit applications? (Online portal, in person, mail, email) Does it vary by jurisdiction?

4.2. Walk us through a typical online portal submission step by step. What do you click, upload, enter?

4.3. For in-person submissions, what's the process? Where do you go? What do you bring?

4.4. How do you confirm that a submission was received and accepted?

4.5. What tracking number or reference do you get? Where do you log it?

4.6. What are the most common submission rejections or issues? (Incomplete forms, wrong format, missing docs)

4.7. How do you handle a jurisdiction you've never submitted to before? What's the research process?

---

## Section 5: Permit Status Tracking & Follow-Up

5.1. After submission, how do you track permit status? What system? How often do you check?

5.2. How are you notified when a permit status changes? (Email, portal notification, you have to check manually?)

5.3. What are all the possible permit statuses you encounter? What does each mean?

5.4. What's the typical timeline from submission to approval? By jurisdiction or type?

5.5. Which jurisdictions are the fastest? Slowest? Most unpredictable?

5.6. How do you follow up on permits that seem stuck? What's the cadence? What do you say?

5.7. How do you escalate a permit that's taking unreasonably long?

5.8. How do you communicate permit status to the PM? How often? What format?

5.9. How do you update HubSpot with permit status changes? What fields do you touch?

---

## Section 6: Permit Corrections & Revisions

6.1. How often do you receive correction requests from AHJs? What percentage of submissions?

6.2. What are the top 10 most common correction requests? For each: what they ask for, who fixes it, how long it takes.

6.3. How are correction requests communicated to you? (Portal, email, letter, phone call?)

6.4. Walk us through the correction process — from receiving the request to resubmission.

6.5. Who handles the correction? You, the design team, or both? How do you coordinate?

6.6. How quickly do corrections need to be turned around? What's the consequence of delays?

6.7. How do you track which corrections are pending, in progress, and resubmitted?

6.8. What correction patterns do you see that could be prevented at the design stage?

6.9. How do you handle an AHJ reviewer who is inconsistent or unreasonable?

7.7. How long does IC application preparation take per project?
