# SOP Questionnaire: Operations Team

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 60–75 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a new ops team member on their first day.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for every day?

1.3. How is the ops team structured? Who handles what? (Scheduling, day-of coordination, Zuper management, inventory, etc.)

1.4. What's the boundary between ops and project management? Where does your ownership start and theirs end?

1.5. Are there tasks that officially belong to another team but ops handles regularly? What and why?

1.6. How does your role change between busy season and slow season?

---

## Section 2: Daily Workflow & Prioritization

2.1. Walk us through your morning routine. What do you check first, second, third when you start your day?

2.2. What systems do you have open all day? What tabs/views are always up?

2.3. How do you prioritize your work when you have scheduling, coordination, and fire-fighting all competing?

2.4. What does a typical day look like in terms of time allocation? (X% scheduling, Y% coordination, Z% problem-solving)

2.5. How do you handle interruptions? What's urgent enough to drop what you're doing?

2.6. What does end-of-day look like? What do you close out or set up for tomorrow?

2.7. What recurring meetings or syncs are on your calendar? Which are useful and which aren't?

---

## Section 3: Installation Scheduling

3.1. How do you decide which projects get scheduled for installation and in what order? What are all the factors?

3.2. Walk us through the exact process of scheduling an install — from deciding "this project is next" to "it's on the calendar."

3.3. How far in advance do you schedule? What's the ideal lead time? What actually happens?

3.4. What makes a project ready to schedule? What are the prerequisites?

3.5. How do you match projects to crews? What factors determine which crew gets which job? (Skill level, geography, system size, roof type, etc.)

3.6. How do you handle crew capacity? How do you know how many jobs a crew can handle per day/week?

3.7. How does geography/travel time factor into scheduling? Do you cluster jobs by area?

3.8. How do you handle the customer communication about scheduling? Who contacts the customer, when, and how?

3.9. What causes reschedules? How often? What's the process when you need to reschedule?

3.10. How do you handle weather-related cancellations? What's the decision process? At what point do you call it?

3.11. What's the process for same-week or next-day scheduling changes?

3.12. How do you balance revenue priority vs first-come-first-served vs customer urgency?

3.13. What's the most stressful part of scheduling? What would make it easier?

---

## Section 4: BOM & Materials Management

4.1. How is the Bill of Materials (BOM) generated for each project? Who creates it? What system?

4.2. How do you verify the BOM is correct before install day? What do you check?

4.3. Where do you check inventory? What system? How accurate is it?

4.4. What happens when a required material is out of stock? What's the process? Who do you notify?

4.5. How are materials ordered? Who has authority to order? What's the approval process?

4.6. How are materials staged for install day? Who pulls them? When? Where are they staged?

4.7. What are the most common BOM errors? What causes them?

4.8. How do you handle substitutions when the specified product isn't available?

4.9. What's the process when a crew discovers they need something not on the BOM?

4.10. How do you track material returns and excess inventory after a job?

---

## Section 5: Zuper Sync & Crew Management

5.1. Walk us through how you use Zuper day-to-day. What's your typical Zuper workflow?

5.2. How are Zuper jobs created? Manually or automatically? What triggers job creation?

5.3. How do you assign crews to Zuper jobs? What's the process? What information do you enter?

5.4. How do you manage crew availability? What tool? How do crews communicate their availability?

5.5. What job categories do you work with in Zuper? (Install, inspection, service, etc.) How do the workflows differ?

5.6. How do you track job status in Zuper? What status transitions matter to you?

5.7. What custom fields do you use in Zuper? Which are important?

5.8. How does information flow between HubSpot and Zuper? What syncs automatically? What do you do manually?

5.9. What breaks in the HubSpot-Zuper sync? What are the common problems?

5.10. How do you handle crew changes (someone calls in sick, crew reassignment)?

5.11. How do you communicate with crew leads? What tool? How often?

5.12. What Zuper reports or views do you rely on?

---

## Section 6: Construction Day Operations

6.1. What's the morning-of checklist before crews go out? What do you verify?

6.2. How do you confirm crews have the right materials loaded?

6.3. How do you brief crews on the day's jobs? What info do they need?

6.4. How do you monitor progress throughout the day? What updates do you get and from whom?

6.5. What triggers a "stop work" or escalation on a job site? Who makes that call?

6.6. How do you handle it when a crew runs into an unexpected issue (roof damage, panel not fitting, electrical problem, customer dispute)?

6.7. What does end-of-day reporting look like from the crews? What do they submit? In what format?

6.8. How do you verify that a job was completed correctly? Is there a quality check?

6.9. What photos or documentation do crews take on-site? Where does that go?

6.10. When a job can't be completed in one day, what's the process for scheduling the return trip?

6.11. How do you handle it when a customer isn't home or denies access?

6.12. What safety protocols exist? How are they enforced?

---

## Section 7: Inspection Coordination

7.1. Who schedules inspections? How is the inspection date determined?

7.2. How do you coordinate with the jurisdiction/AHJ for inspection scheduling?

7.3. What do you do to prepare for an inspection? Is there a pre-inspection checklist?

7.4. Who attends the inspection? Is someone always on-site? Who?

7.5. How are you notified of results (pass/fail/conditional)?

7.6. When an inspection fails, what's the process? Who does what? How is the correction handled?

7.7. What are the top 5 reasons inspections fail? Are any preventable?

7.8. How do you schedule re-inspections? What's the turnaround?

7.9. What jurisdictions are the hardest to work with for inspections? Why?

7.10. How do you communicate inspection results to the PM and customer?

---

## Section 8: Service & Warranty Calls

8.1. How do service/warranty calls come in? Through what channels?

8.2. How are service calls triaged? Who does initial assessment? What determines priority?

8.3. What's the typical response time target? What actually happens?

8.4. How are service jobs created in Zuper? Is it different from install jobs?

8.5. How do you determine if a service issue is warranty-covered vs billable?

8.6. What's the scheduling process for service calls? Same as installs or different?

8.7. What equipment/parts do service crews need? How do they get them?

8.8. How do you track service call resolution? What constitutes "resolved"?

8.9. What are the most common service call types? (Monitoring issues, equipment failure, damage, etc.)

8.10. How do repeat issues get flagged? Is there a pattern-detection process?

---

## Section 9: Detach & Reset (D&R) and Roofing

9.1. How does a D&R project differ from a standard install in terms of ops workflow?

9.2. What additional coordination is required for D&R? (Roofer scheduling, equipment storage, etc.)

9.3. How do you coordinate with roofing contractors? What's the communication process?

9.4. What's the scheduling sequence for D&R? (Detach → roof → reset — how do you manage that timeline?)

9.5. How do you handle equipment storage between detach and reset? Where does it go? How is it tracked?

9.6. For standalone roofing projects, how does the process differ from solar?

9.7. What are the unique challenges of D&R that don't exist in standard installs?

10.4. What data do you wish you had easy access to but currently don't?

10.6. What manual processes could be automated?

11.2. How do you communicate with crew leads in the field? What tool? Real-time or batched?

11.3. How do you handle urgent communications during the day?

11.4. What information do you need from other teams that you consistently don't get on time?

11.5. What cross-team meetings do you participate in? Which are valuable?

## Section 12: Exceptions, Edge Cases & Crisis Management

12.1. What are the top 5 "fires" you deal with regularly? How do you handle each one?

12.2. What happens when a crew calls in and says they can't complete a job? What's your immediate response?

12.3. How do you handle a day when multiple jobs have problems simultaneously?

12.4. What's the escalation chain when something goes seriously wrong on a job site?

12.5. How do you handle equipment damage (solar panels broken, inverter DOA, etc.)?

12.6. What happens when a customer refuses to let the crew work after they arrive?

12.7. Describe the worst day you've had on ops in the last year. What happened? What would have prevented it?
