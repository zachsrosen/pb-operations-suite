# SOP Questionnaire: Project Management

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 60–90 minutes (this is the longest questionnaire because PMs touch the full lifecycle)
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a new PM on their first day. That's the level of detail we're after.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. How many active projects do you typically manage at once? What's the comfortable number vs the "too many" number?

1.3. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for? What are you the "last line of defense" on?

1.4. Where does your ownership start (what triggers you to pick up a project) and where does it end (what's the final thing you do before it's off your plate)?

1.5. What parts of the process do you own end-to-end vs coordinate with other teams?

1.6. Are there tasks that officially belong to another team but you end up doing regularly? What and why?

1.7. What gets deprioritized when you're overloaded? What's the first thing to slip?

1.8. How do you triage your day when everything feels urgent?

## Section 2: Deal Intake & Initial Review

2.1. How are you notified that a new project has been assigned to you? What system, what notification?

2.2. What's the first thing you do when a new deal lands on your desk? Walk us through the first 15 minutes.

2.3. What do you review on the deal record? What specific fields, documents, and notes do you check?

2.4. What makes a deal "ready" for you to start working on? What's the minimum viable set of information?

2.5. What are the most common problems with deals when they arrive from sales? List the top 5.

2.6. When a deal isn't ready, what do you do? Who do you go to? How do you track that it needs something?

2.7. Do you have a formal intake checklist, or is it from memory? If from memory, what would be on the checklist?

2.8. How do you prioritize new deals against your existing workload? What factors matter?

2.9. What's the first communication you have with the customer? When does it happen and what do you say?

2.10. What deal properties do you fill in or update during intake?

## Section 3: Site Survey Coordination

3.1. How do you determine if a site survey is needed? Is it always needed?

3.2. Walk us through the exact steps to get a survey scheduled — what system, what clicks, what info?

3.3. How do you coordinate survey timing with the customer? With the survey crew?

3.4. What special instructions or notes do you provide for the survey crew? How do you communicate them?

3.5. How do you know when the survey is done? Who notifies you and how?

3.6. What do you review from the survey results? What are you looking for? What makes you raise a flag?

3.7. What problems come back from surveys most often (access issues, shading, roof condition, etc.)? How do you handle each one?

3.8. When does a project need a re-survey? What triggers it? How often does that happen?

3.9. What's the target timeline from deal intake to survey complete? What actually happens?

3.10. What's your involvement during the survey itself? Do you attend? Monitor? Wait for results?
