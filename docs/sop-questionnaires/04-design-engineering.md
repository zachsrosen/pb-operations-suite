# SOP Questionnaire: Design & Engineering Team

> **Purpose**: Help us document and improve your standard operating procedures. Be as detailed as possible — the more specific your answers, the better our SOPs will be. If a question doesn't apply, write "N/A" and explain why. If you're unsure, give your best understanding and flag it.
>
> **Time estimate**: 45–60 minutes
>
> **Tip**: For each question, think about what you'd tell a new designer on their first day.

---

## Section 1: Your Role & Responsibilities

1.1. What is your job title, and how long have you been in this role at PB?

1.2. What are the top 5 things you are personally responsible for?

1.3. How is the design team structured? How many designers? Do people specialize (residential vs commercial, ground mount vs roof, battery, etc.)?

1.4. What's the boundary between design and permitting? Between design and project management?

1.5. What parts of the project do you own end-to-end vs collaborate on?

1.6. Are there tasks that aren't officially yours but you handle regularly? What and why?

1.7. How do you prioritize when you have more design requests than capacity?

---

## Section 2: Design Queue & Intake

2.1. How do new design requests arrive? What system notifies you? What's the trigger?

2.2. What information do you need to start a design? What's the minimum viable set?

2.3. What information is commonly missing or wrong when a design request comes in? What do you do when it is?

2.4. How do you prioritize the design queue? FIFO? Revenue? PM urgency? Something else?

2.5. What's the target turnaround from "request received" to "design complete"? What actually happens?

2.6. How do you track your queue and workload? What tool/system?

2.7. When you're overloaded, what happens? Who decides what gets bumped? How is that communicated?

---

## Section 3: Site Survey Data & Assessment

3.1. What data do you receive from the site survey? In what format? Through what system?

3.2. What do you review first when you receive survey data? What are you looking for?

3.3. What makes for "good" survey data vs "bad" survey data? What's commonly missing or wrong?

3.4. When survey data is insufficient, what do you do? Who do you contact? How long does it take to get what you need?

3.5. How do you assess roof suitability? What factors matter (pitch, azimuth, shading, condition, material, obstacles)?

3.6. How do you assess electrical panel capacity and service requirements?

3.7. What site conditions cause you to change your design approach significantly?

3.8. How do you handle sites with complex shading (trees, neighboring buildings, etc.)?

---

## Section 4: System Design Process

4.1. Walk us through your complete design process step by step — from opening the survey data to delivering a finished design.

4.2. What software tools do you use for design? (Aurora, AutoCAD, Helioscope, SketchUp, etc.) For what purpose?

4.3. How do you determine the optimal system size? What factors drive that decision?

4.4. How do you select equipment? (Panel manufacturer/model, inverter type, racking system, etc.) What drives those choices?

4.5. How do you decide on panel placement and layout? What constraints do you work with?

4.6. How do you handle battery/storage system design? What's different about those designs?

4.7. How do you handle ground-mount designs? What additional considerations exist?

4.8. How do you ensure your design meets electrical code requirements (NEC)?

4.9. How do you handle fire setback and access pathway requirements? Do these vary by jurisdiction?

4.10. What structural considerations factor into your design? (Roof loading, attachment points, wind/snow loads)

4.11. How do you handle designs for flat commercial roofs vs pitched residential roofs?

4.12. What production estimates/modeling do you do? What tool? How accurate is it?

---

## Section 5: Plan Set Creation

5.1. What goes into a complete plan set? List every page/sheet and what it contains.

5.2. How long does it take to create a complete plan set from scratch? What are the time-intensive parts?

5.3. Do you use templates? What templates? How are they maintained?

5.4. What engineering calculations are included in the plan set? (String sizing, voltage drop, conduit fill, structural, etc.)

5.5. How do you handle single-line diagrams? What software? What level of detail?

5.6. What notes and specifications are standard on every plan set?

5.7. How do you handle AHJ-specific requirements that vary by jurisdiction?

5.8. How do you create site plans and layouts? What data sources do you use?

5.9. What's your quality check before delivering a plan set? Self-review? Peer review?

5.10. How do you version plan sets? How do you track which version is current?

---

## Section 6: Design Approval (DA) Package

6.1. What do you produce for the DA package that goes to the customer? How is it different from the full plan set?

6.2. How do you create the production estimate that accompanies the DA?

6.3. What visual materials do you create for the customer? (3D renders, layout diagrams, etc.)

6.4. How long does it take to produce the DA package after the design is done?

6.5. Who reviews the DA package before it goes to the PM/customer?

---

## Section 7: Revisions

7.1. What triggers a design revision? List every type you encounter. (Customer change, permit correction, IC requirement, field discovery, etc.)

7.2. How do revision requests come in? What system? From whom?

7.3. What information do you need with a revision request to execute it efficiently?

7.4. How do you prioritize revisions vs new designs? What's the rule?

7.5. What's the typical turnaround on a revision? Does it depend on the type?

7.6. How do you track revision counts and types? What system?

7.7. What are the most common revision reasons? Which are preventable?

7.8. How do you handle a revision that fundamentally changes the system design vs a minor tweak?

7.9. How do you update the plan set version when a revision is made? How do you ensure the old version isn't used?

7.10. What's the most frustrating type of revision to deal with? Why?

---

## Section 8: AHJ & Utility Knowledge

8.1. How do you keep track of jurisdiction-specific requirements? Is there a database, spreadsheet, or is it in people's heads?

8.2. What are the most common AHJ-specific requirements that affect your designs?

8.3. How do you learn about a new AHJ's requirements when you encounter one for the first time?

8.4. How do you stay current when an AHJ changes their requirements?

8.5. What utility-specific requirements affect your designs? (Interconnection specs, meter requirements, etc.)

8.6. What jurisdictions are the hardest to design for? Why?

8.7. What AHJ knowledge do you wish was documented that currently isn't?

---

## Section 9: Quality Control & Review

9.1. What's your quality review process before a plan set is finalized?

9.2. Do you do peer reviews? How? Who reviews what?

9.3. What are the most common errors that get caught in review?

9.4. What errors make it through review and get caught later (by the AHJ, in the field, etc.)? What would prevent them?

9.5. How do you handle it when a plan set error is discovered after construction has started?

9.6. What design standards or checklists do you use internally?

---

## Section 10: Third-Party Design / Outsourced Design

10.1. Does PB use any third-party or outsourced design services (e.g., Vishtik)? For what types of work?

10.2. How do you communicate with third-party designers? What system? What information do you provide?

10.3. How do you review third-party designs before accepting them?

10.4. What problems arise with third-party designs? What's commonly wrong or missing?

10.5. How do you handle revisions on third-party designs?

10.6. What's the turnaround expectation for third-party work?

---

## Section 11: PE Stamps & Engineering Review

11.1. What projects require a PE stamp? What determines that?

11.2. How do you submit designs for PE review? What system/process?

11.3. What does the PE typically flag or require changes on?

11.4. How long does PE review take? What's the turnaround?

11.5. How do you handle PE-required changes?

---

## Section 12: Tools & Software

12.1. List every tool and software you use. For each: what you use it for, proficiency level, how critical (1–5), how well it works (1–5).

12.2. What tools do you wish you had that would make your job easier?

12.3. What manual processes could be automated or streamlined with better tools?

12.4. How do you store and organize design files? Where do completed plan sets live?

12.5. What naming conventions do you use for files?

---

## Section 13: Collaboration & Communication

13.1. How do you communicate with PMs about design status and questions?

13.2. How do you communicate with the permitting team?

13.3. How do you communicate with field crews when they have design questions on-site?

13.4. What meetings or syncs do you participate in? Which are useful?

13.5. When you're blocked on a design because you need information, what do you do? Who do you ask?

---

## Section 14: Exceptions & Edge Cases

14.1. What are the most unusual or complex designs you've worked on? What made them hard?

14.2. How do you handle a project that can't fit the customer's requested system size? What's the conversation?

14.3. How do you handle designs for older homes with outdated electrical?

14.4. What do you do when the survey data contradicts what the customer told sales?

14.5. How do you handle designs for properties with multiple structures or detached garages?

15.1. How many designs can you complete per week realistically? What's the bottleneck?

15.2. What metrics are you measured on?

15.3. How do you track your throughput and turnaround time?

15.4. What would increase your output without sacrificing quality?

16.1. What would a new designer need to know in their first two weeks?

17.1. What's the single biggest bottleneck in the design process?

17.3. What works really well in the design workflow?
