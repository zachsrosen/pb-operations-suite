## Snow Dog (Solar Pad-Style Snow Guard)

Individual pad-style snow guard that clamps between solar panels in the horizontal joints of a PV array. Holds and breaks up sliding snow mass to prevent roof snow avalanches. Part of Alpine's three-component solar snow management system.

### Manufacturer Quantity Rules

Per Alpine's published installation guidelines:

- **Portrait orientation**: 2 Snow Dogs per module
- **Landscape (horizontal) orientation**: 3 Snow Dogs per module

These are installed in the horizontal joints between panel rows, spaced evenly across the module width.

### Roof Pitch & Snow Load

- Required for solar arrays on **2/12 pitch or greater** in snowy regions
- Glass surfaces shed snow more aggressively than asphalt shingles (no granule friction)
- Meltwater acts as lubricant, increasing avalanche velocity
- If snow accumulates to depths greater than 6 inches, manual clearing is recommended
- Reference standard: ASCE 7-16 snow load provisions

### Three-Component System

Alpine recommends a layered approach for full snow management:

1. **Snow Dog** (pad-style) -- between panels, friction points across array surface
2. **Solar SnowMax Universal** (fence-style) -- mounted at bottom edge of array as barrier
3. **SSG-313** (pipe-style, roof-mounted) -- installed below array when minimum 24" landing zone is available

Snow Dogs can be installed alone but achieve best performance when paired with SnowMax and/or SSG-313.

### Installation Notes

- Clamps onto aluminum module frames in horizontal joints
- Does not penetrate panels -- no warranty impact
- Designed to slow snow/ice evacuation, not fully prevent it
- Critical areas to protect: points of egress, walkways, driveways, decks, gathering areas
